# Landing Mode — building marketing & entry screens

Read this when building anything a logged-out visitor sees: landing pages, hero sections, pricing, login/signup, announcements, product/news pages.

Landing mode is **editorial and spacious**. It makes a statement. Big serif display carries the page, negative space does the heavy lifting, and the few interactive elements are confident and minimal.

## The frame

- Same warm paper background (`#faf9f5`). Never cold white.
- **Minimal top navbar:** orange asterisk + "Claude" wordmark on the left; a sparse set of nav links (often with small chevrons for dropdowns) in the center/right in sans; on the far right one quiet **outline "Contact sales"** button next to one **solid black "Try Claude"** button. That two-button pairing (quiet outline + solid black) is the canonical CTA lockup.
- An optional thin secondary sub-nav strip can sit under the main nav (a section label left, an "Explore here" affordance right), low-contrast.
- Sections are tall and breathe. Few elements per viewport. Strong vertical rhythm.

## The hero (the signature moment)

- **Huge serif display headline**, often two lines, set large with tight-ish line-height and normal/medium weight — not heavy bold. This is the single most recognizable landing element ("Pense rápido, construa mais rápido"). Let it be big and let it have room.
- A short **sans subtitle** beneath in a muted tone, one line, supporting not competing.
- Layout is frequently **split**: text + primary action on one side, a product visual/screenshot on the other — with lots of margin around both. Or fully centered for pages like pricing where a single serif title sits centered up top.
- CTAs in the hero are few and decisive.

## Buttons (landing)

- **Primary = solid black** (`#141413`), white text, **rectangular with a gentle radius** (not pill). Confident, simple. e.g. "Continuar com e-mail", "Try Claude".
- **Secondary = outline / ghost**, hairline border on the warm paper, dark text. e.g. "Contact sales", "Baixar aplicativo para desktop" (which can carry a small leading icon).
- A provider button (e.g. "Continuar com Google") is a neutral outline button with the provider glyph + label, full-width inside a form card.

## Forms / login cards

- A single **rounded card**, near-white, hairline border, generous padding, centered or in the hero's text column.
- Inside, stacked full-width: a provider button → a quiet "OU" divider (muted text with hairline rules on each side) → a labeled input (rounded, hairline border, muted placeholder like "Digite seu e-mail") → a full-width **solid black** submit.
- A tiny muted legal line beneath with an underlined link ("Ao continuar, você reconhece a Política de Privacidade...").

## Pricing pages

- Centered **serif display title** ("Pricing") up top, lots of space.
- A small **segmented toggle** (Individual / Team & Enterprise / API) centered below — pill container, the active segment gets a white raised pill, inactive segments are muted text.
- **Three plan cards** side by side: each is near-white, big radius, hairline border, generous padding, well separated.
  - Top: a small thin-stroke line glyph (a little plant/branch motif).
  - Plan name in **serif** (Free / Pro / Max), one-line muted tagline beneath.
  - Big price (sans, bold) with a muted billing note.
  - A full-width **solid black "Try Claude"** button.
  - A hairline divider, then a feature list: each row a small check glyph + sans label, with a bold "Everything in X, plus:" lead-in. Generous line spacing.
- Cards are equal-weight and quiet; differentiation is by content, not by loud color or a "most popular" scream. (A subtle emphasis on one card is fine — a faint border tint — but keep it restrained.)

## Content / announcement pages

- A serif section title paired with a small geometric/line motif (e.g. a little triangle) on the left, and a clean list of entries on the right.
- Each entry: a **serif item title**, a small line icon + muted category label beneath ("Announcement", "Product"). Lots of vertical space between entries; no boxes — the list breathes.
- A small floating **"Latest news" toast/card** can sit bottom-right: near-white, big radius, soft, with a title, a line of muted copy, and a small solid black "Learn more" button.

## Landing-mode tone summary

Big serif statement + huge negative space + minimal confident controls. Black solid primary, outline secondary, orange only in the wordmark/asterisk. If the page feels busy, shrink the element count and grow the type and the margins — restraint reads as premium here.
